Happiness Scores of Countries

The World Happiness Report is an annually released report ranking the happiness of countries based on several quality-of-life indicators of the people of each nation. It was first introduced in 2012 and has been released annually since 2013. The report outlines happiness of each country and the causes of happiness and misery. This helps the policy making bodies in countries to create their policies to address their citizens needs and improve their happiness.

Correlation between Happiness score and GDP per Capita

This chart shows the distribution of happiness scores in each region. As you can see in the chart Western Europe is mostly distributed in the 7 to 7.5 range. In fact, in the 2023 rankings Finland was named as the happiest country in the world. Meanwhile, Sub-Saharan Africa countries are distributed in the 3.5 to 5 in the happiness score range.

Gross Domestic Product per Capita is the Gross Domestic Product, which is the total value of all goods and services produced within a country's borders in a specific period, usually a year or a quarter divided by the population. High GDP per capita generally indicates a higher standard of living and more economic opportunities for the residents of a country, while low GDP per capita can suggest lower living standards and potentially higher poverty levels. As you can see in the chart countries with higher happiness seemingly has higher GDP per capita.
